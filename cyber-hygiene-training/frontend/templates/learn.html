🛡️ Cybersecurity Awareness

Learn to recognize and defend against online threats

🎣 How Phishing Works

Phishing is a cyberattack where criminals impersonate trusted entities to trick you into revealing sensitive information. They use fake emails, messages, and websites that look legitimate. The goal is to steal passwords, payment cards, or install malware.

🎰 Fake Lotteries

Scammers announce you've won prizes you never entered to win. They request personal information or small "processing fees." Legitimate lotteries never ask winners to pay upfront or provide banking details via unsolicited messages.

📱 Telegram Scams

Telegram's popularity makes it a prime target. Scammers send prize messages, fake investment opportunities, or impersonate support staff. Always verify bot identities and never share verification codes through Telegram.

📷 QR-Code Attacks (Quishing)

Malicious QR codes redirect to phishing sites or trigger malware downloads. Always preview URLs before visiting. Be especially cautious with QR codes in public places or unsolicited messages.

💳 Fake Payment Pages

Scam websites mimic real banking and payment interfaces. Warning signs include suspicious domains, missing security indicators, poor grammar, and requests for unusual information like SMS codes alongside card details.

🧠 Social Engineering

Attackers manipulate human psychology rather than technical systems. They exploit trust, authority, urgency, and curiosity. The best defense is skepticism and verification through official channels.

⏰ Urgency Tactics

Countdown timers, "limited spots," and "act now" messages pressure you to skip verification. Legitimate organizations give you time to make informed decisions.

😨 Fear Tactics

Messages threatening account closure, legal action, or lost prizes create panic. Scammers want you to act before thinking. Pause, verify, and contact organizations directly.

🎁 Reward Manipulation

Too-good-to-be-true prizes exploit greed and excitement. If you didn't enter a contest, you didn't win. Free luxury cars and millions in cash are almost always scams.

📲 OTP Theft

SMS verification codes are the keys to your accounts. Scammers who obtain your card number will call or message asking for the code. Banks never ask for your OTP — ever.

🪪 Identity Theft

Personal information collected through scams enables identity fraud — opening accounts, taking loans, or committing crimes in your name. Guard your name, ID numbers, and contact details carefully.

💸 Card Fraud

Entering card details on fake sites gives criminals everything needed for unauthorized purchases. Combined with OTP theft, they can drain accounts within minutes.
